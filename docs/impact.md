# ClassBridge — Impact

## Local Impact: Multilingual Students

In communities where English is the language of instruction but not the native language, students face a double challenge: they must learn new concepts while simultaneously processing unfamiliar language. ClassBridge directly addresses this by providing:

- **Tamil and Hindi translations** alongside English explanations
- **Side-by-side bilingual views** so students can compare and learn
- **Grade-level-appropriate language** that matches the student's reading ability

This means a Tamil-speaking student struggling with an English biology worksheet can instantly get a Tamil translation at their reading level, helping them focus on the science rather than decoding the language.

## Global Accessibility Impact

Over 2 billion students worldwide learn in a language other than their mother tongue. ClassBridge's architecture supports adding languages easily, making it scalable to serve:

- Students in multilingual countries (India, Nigeria, Philippines, etc.)
- Immigrant and refugee students learning in a new language
- Students in rural areas with limited English exposure

## Why Simpler Language Matters

Research consistently shows that reading comprehension drops significantly when text exceeds a student's reading level. ClassBridge's AI-powered simplification:

- Rewrites complex sentences into shorter, clearer ones
- Replaces academic jargon with everyday vocabulary
- Provides real-life analogies that connect abstract concepts to familiar experiences
- Preserves the original meaning while making it accessible

## Why Read-Aloud Support Matters

Many students with learning differences (dyslexia, ADHD, visual processing issues) benefit significantly from hearing text read aloud. ClassBridge's browser-native TTS:

- Provides immediate auditory reinforcement of written content
- Supports pronunciation of complex scientific terms
- Gives students control over pace (pause, replay, stop)
- Works without external software or internet connectivity

## Supporting Under-Resourced Schools

Schools with limited access to tutoring, special education, or one-on-one teacher support can use ClassBridge as a supplementary learning assistant:

- Teachers can generate simplified versions of their worksheets instantly
- Students can self-serve when teachers are unavailable
- Parents can access the Parent/Teacher Summary to understand how to help at home
- No additional hardware or software required — works in any modern browser

## Measuring Impact

ClassBridge can help track:
- How many students use simplified vs. original content
- Which topics are most commonly simplified
- Practice question performance over time
- Language preference patterns across regions

This data can inform curriculum designers about where simplified materials are most needed.
